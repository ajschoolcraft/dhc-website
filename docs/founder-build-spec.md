Digital Health Counsel Website — Initial Build Spec

1. Project Overview

Build a public-facing website for Digital Health Counsel that serves two primary purposes:

Establish Digital Health Counsel as the anchor platform for the annual Digital Health Counsel AI Summit, including its mission, audience, history, prior events, speakers, sponsors, photos, and thought-leadership positioning.

Serve as the application and registration hub for DHC26, the 2026 Digital Health Counsel AI Summit, to be held December 2–3, 2026, at Bell Harbor Conference Center in Seattle.

The site should feel polished, professional, selective, and credible for an audience of health system general counsel, product counsel, privacy/compliance leaders, legal operations leaders, digital health companies, AI companies, regulators, academics, law firms, consultants, insurers, brokers, and technology sponsors.

The site should not feel like a generic conference ticketing page or vendor showcase. It should communicate that DHC26 isurated professional summit focused on the practical legal infrastructure required for healthcare AI.

2. Core Positioning

Digital Health Counsel

Digital Health Counsel is a convening platform for leaders working at the intersection of healthcare, technology, artificial intelligence, law, governance, privacy, data strategy, and innovation.

DHC26 Working Theme

From Promise to Proof: The Legal Operating System for Healthcare AI

Core Message

Healthcare AI has moved beyond the question of whether the technology is impressive. The next phase is about evidence, governance, contracting, liability allocation, data rights, product counsel, consumer-facing AI, legal operations, and institutional trust.

DHC26 should be positioned as the curated summit for the lawyers and leaders responsible for making healthcare AI provable, governable, contractable, and trustworthy.

4. Application and Registration Workflow

The website should not initially handle full public registration. It should serve as the public front door for applications.

Workflow

User visits site.

User clicks Apply to Participate.

User completes application form.

Application is stored in the back-end database.

DHC team reviews application.

Applicant receives one of the following responses:

Accepted — complimentary registration

Accepted — reduced-fee registration

Accepted — paid registration

Speaker/moderator under consideration

Sponsor follow-up

Waitlist

Declined or no fit at this time

Accepted applicants receive a private registration link or instructions.

Registration/payment is handled separately from the public application page.

Confirmed attendees are tracked in the back-end database.
